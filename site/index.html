PlayHQ

The PlayStation capture experience — on your PC. Native C++, portable, controller-first.

Tap Share            → take a screenshot
Hold Share (2 s)     → save the last 5 minutes of gameplay
PS button            → open the GameHQ overlay above your game
PS again / Circle    → back to the game, exactly where you were

What is GameHQ?

GameHQ is the missing capture system for PC gaming. Consoles solved this years ago: one button saves the moment, another opens a gallery right on top of the game, and everything is neatly filed per title. On PC you get a patchwork instead — Game Bar popups, Steam screenshots in one folder, NVIDIA clips in another, and an alt-tab every time you want to check what you just recorded. GameHQ replaces that patchwork with a single, console-grade experience.

At its heart is an always-on replay buffer: while you play, GameHQ quietly encodes the last five minutes of gameplay (1080p30, H.264, system audio) into a rolling ring of segments on disk — low RAM, crash-safe, nothing to start or stop. Something great happens? Hold Share for two seconds and the moment is already saved, exported as a clean MP4, thumbnailed, and filed under the game you were playing. A quick tap of Share grabs a full-quality screenshot the same way.

Reviewing captures never pulls you out of the game. Press the PS button and GameHQ's overlay slides in above the running game — a couch-readable gallery you drive entirely with the controller: D-pad to browse, X to play, Triangle to favorite, Circle to go straight back to the action with focus restored exactly where it was. Favorites are protected from cleanup forever, storage limits keep clip folders from swallowing your disk, and GameHQ can watch your existing Game Bar, Steam, NVIDIA and OBS folders so every capture you already own lives in the same gallery.

Technically, GameHQ is deliberately old-school in the best way: a portable native C++20/Qt 6 app — no Electron, no services, no account. It uses Windows Graphics Capture and Media Foundation directly, never injects into game processes (anti-cheat safe), runs from a folder if you want it to, and sits in the tray with subtle, PlayStation-style sounds as its only presence.

How it works

1

Play

GameHQ detects the game and spins up the rolling replay buffer in the background.

2

Capture

Tap Share for a screenshot, hold it two seconds to save the last five minutes.

3

Browse

Press PS — the overlay gallery opens above the game, fully controller-driven.

4

Return

Circle closes the overlay and hands focus back to the game, right where you left it.

Why GameHQ

In-game overlay gallery

Browse screenshots and clips above the running game with your controller — no alt-tab, no desktop app in your face.

Always-ready replay buffer

A rolling 1080p30 buffer records the last 5 minutes. One hold of the Share button saves the moment — after it happened.

Organized around games

Captures are grouped per game automatically, with favorites that are never auto-deleted and clean per-game folders.

Native & portable

C++20 + Qt 6, no Electron. Runs from a folder, sits quietly in the tray, plays subtle console-style sounds.

DualSense-first input

Share tap/hold, PS toggle, full pad navigation — plus rebindable keyboard hotkeys as a reliable fallback.

Your existing captures too

Watches Game Bar, Steam, NVIDIA and OBS folders so everything lives in one gallery — without touching your files.
